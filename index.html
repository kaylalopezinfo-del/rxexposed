📚 Data Sources & Methodology
All data publicly available from government agencies
US Drug Pricing
① GoodRx Public Price Data
Retail cash prices at CVS, Walgreens, Rite Aid and 70,000+ US pharmacies. Updated continuously.
→ goodrx.com
② CMS Maximum Fair Price (MFP)
Medicare-negotiated prices under the Inflation Reduction Act. Published quarterly by CMS.
→ cms.gov/inflation-reduction-act
③ CMS Average Sales Price (ASP)
Manufacturer-reported average selling prices for Medicare Part B drugs. Published quarterly.
→ cms.gov/medicare/payment/part-b-drugs
International Pricing
④ NHS England Drug Tariff
Official monthly drug pricing published by NHS Business Services Authority.
→ nhsbsa.nhs.uk/drug-tariff
⑤ Health Canada Drug Product Database
Canadian government drug pricing and product information registry.
→ health-products.canada.ca
⑥ PBS Australia
Australian Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme subsidized drug prices. Published monthly.
→ pbs.gov.au
⑦ OECD Health Statistics
Annual drug price comparisons across 38 countries.
→ oecd.org/health/health-statistics
Doctor & Hospital Data
⑧ CMS Open Payments Program
Federal database of payments from pharmaceutical and device companies to physicians. Mandated by the Physician Payments Sunshine Act. Program Year 2024: 16.16 million records, $13.18 billion total.
→ openpaymentsdata.cms.gov
⑨ Medicare Part D Prescribers by Provider and Drug
CMS dataset showing prescription counts and costs per provider per drug. Medicare patients only — does not represent all prescribing activity.
→ data.cms.gov
Patent Data
⑩ FDA Orange Book
Official FDA database of approved drug products with patent numbers and expiration dates. Updated monthly. 1,577 patent records loaded.
→ fda.gov/drugs/orange-book-data-files
Methodology
US retail prices = GoodRx cash prices without insurance, verified June 2026. International prices = government-published reimbursement or national health system rates. Conflict detection matches company names in CMS Open Payments against brand/generic names in Medicare Part D prescribing data. Financial relationships between physicians and manufacturers do not imply wrongdoing. RxExposed is an independent tool, not affiliated with any pharmaceutical company or government agency.
